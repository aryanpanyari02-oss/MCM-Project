Age Limit for Admission

Maximum age limit is 23 years (25 years for girls) on 1st July of the year concerned for 1st year UG programmes.

Relaxation of 3 years is granted for SC/ST candidates. VC can grant 6 months relaxation for genuine reasons.

Minimum Percentage of Marks Required

Eligibility criteria for admission into 1st Year / 1st Semester undergraduate programmes.

B.A. 1st Year

  • 33% marks at +2 level are required.
  • Students opting for Mathematics must have passed Mathematics at +2 level with either 45% in aggregate or 45% marks in Mathematics.

B.Sc. 1st Year

  • 45% marks at +2 level in four subjects (including English).
  • Applicable for both Medical and Non-Medical streams.

B.Com 1st Year

  • 40% in +2 for students who passed +2 under Commerce Group.
  • 33% in +2 is required for girl students.
  • Arts and Science Students with 45% marks can join, provided they passed Math/Economics at +2 level.

BCA 1st Semester

  • 40% in +2 for all streams.
  • Students from Vocational stream are eligible for admission.

Note: Relaxation of 5% marks in the minimum eligibility required will be allowed for SC/ST candidates in B.Sc./B.Com 1st year/BCA 1st Semester.

The students of National Open School will be admitted provided they have secured requisite marks in all the five subjects.

Other Eligibility Conditions

Important rules and regulations for admission into the college.

A

Candidate furnishing any wrong information will be punished with cancellation of admission/registration. Suitable disciplinary and legal action will be taken against him/her and dues paid will be forfeited.

B

All admissions are provisional till these are confirmed/approved by the University and the college will assume no responsibility for non-approval of a particular case for any reason, including equivalence cases.

C

In the absence of actual marks sheet from Univ/Board in respect of +2 or equivalent exam where the result is shown in grades/standards the minimum percentage of marks for the each subject given in standard will be taken into consideration for determining eligibility.

D

The admission schedule notified by the university will be strictly adhered to. No relaxation will be given in this regard.

E

The decision of the Principal will be final in the matter of admission and he has the power to refuse or terminate any admission without assigning any reason.
